Income vs Party Identification - Statistical Testing

This project examines how annual household income influences political party preferences among ethnic minorities in the UK. Analyzing 2010 survey data from five minority groups, it identifies income-based patterns in party affiliation, with higher earners leaning toward Conservatives and lower-income groups favoring Labour or remaining undecided. These findings can help political parties tailor strategies to better engage minority communities based on income differences.
